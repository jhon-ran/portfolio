About me

Hi, I'm Jhonnatan, a first generation PhD in linguistics and a postdoctoral researcher at the SeDyL (CNRS) in Paris. My interest in language goes beyond any theory and I specialize in variation, documentation and revitalization of critically endangered languages, as well as speech processing for low-resourced languages. I'm a mixe-zoque specialist and I help communities and organizations to develop language and software projects.

I do software development and I love all things languages, both human and computer. Over the last 7 years, I have been an active collaborator in a community-driven language project in rural Mexico. I'm an avid science communicator and I firmly believe that science should be opened and shared with everyone.
